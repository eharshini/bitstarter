The site for managing shared housing and making life more awesome. Live better, live together. The idea: The idea for clubhouse came to me after moving back in with housemates following an 8 month stint in an apartment on my own. I had lived in shared housing since I moved out of my parents house when I finally got a decent job, I decided to get my own place. I was surprised to find that while I had none of the difficulties of shared living, I also had none of the joys and community of that lifestyle. So I spent a few months on craigslist and found a few housemates that I liked. I moved in and life was all around better, but a few pain points still existed. I realized that there were some things that were just part of living in shared housing that I would have to learn to deal with. So I started finding effective ways to deal with them. I got a big whiteboard where we could leave messages for each other (this was huge). I got a shared calendar. But Also, I pushed for us to come up with a house name. For me, this had become an important part of sharing a house. In my experience houses with a name seemed to work better.

The design: Designing the product has been fun. It has been designed and re-designed more times than I can count. I did a lot of white board drawings with different friends, I did a lot of sketches in drawing books. The biggest issue was finding a way to focus the idea. An important part of the idea from the start has been taking care of the landlord. Indeed, I found out a lot of things that landlords would want. Basically anything that would help them find better housemates and get rent on time was something they were willing to pay for. But I ended up with product designs that looked unfocused and scattered. Was I serving landlords or was I serving the housemates? In our efforts to focus the project and truly validate the idea, we decided that we needed to do one or the other. We decide to go with serving the housemates. Quite simply, I'm more interested in serving housemate. These are the people that create the community, that make the house work.
